# Invited Expert: Expectations and Criteria

*Pointer Events Working Group*

This document outlines the participation requirements and evaluation criteria for Invited Experts within the Pointer Events Working Group. Individuals may be invited by the Working Group Chairs in accordance with the [W3C Process for Invited Experts](https://www.w3.org/policies/process/#invited-expert-wg).

## Expectations

We welcome individuals dedicated to advancing and improving the Pointer Events. To maintain active status, participants are expected to contribute consistently through the following actions:

* *Active Engagement:* Participate regularly with other members via the group’s primary communication channels.

* *Substantive Contributions:* Submit and refine proposals to advance current work items.

* *Information Sync:* Stay informed on group progress by reviewing meeting minutes, mailing list discussions, and GitHub issues.

* *Administrative Support:* Assist with "scribing" (note-taking) during meetings.

*Note on Tenure:* New Invited Experts are subject to a six-month trial period. Extensions or renewals are contingent upon active participation; inactive or non-contributing experts may not be extended or renewed.

## Criteria for Selection

The Working Group Chairs and Team Contacts evaluate Invited Expert applications based on the following non-exhaustive criteria:

* *Compliance:* Full adherence to the [W3C Invited Expert conditions and requirements](https://www.w3.org/policies/process/#invited-expert-wg).

* *Conduct:* Demonstrated ability to follow the [W3C Code of Conduct](https://www.w3.org/policies/code-of-conduct/).

* *Track Record:* Prior participation and contributions within the group’s public channels.
